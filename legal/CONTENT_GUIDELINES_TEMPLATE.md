## What's allowed

- Original content you created or have explicit rights to distribute
- Short films, documentaries, series episodes, music videos, vlogs,
  tutorials, and similar creative or informational video work
- Content that uses licensed/royalty-free music or footage with proof of
  license available on request

## What's not allowed

**Copyright violations.** Content using music, clips, or footage you don't
have rights to, even briefly, even if attributed. This includes
unmodified re-uploads of others' work.

**Hate speech and harassment.** Content that attacks, threatens, or
demeans people based on race, ethnicity, religion, gender, sexual
orientation, disability, or similar protected characteristics. Content
that targets or harasses a specific individual.

**Content endangering minors.** Any sexualization of minors, real or
fictional, is grounds for immediate permanent ban and may be reported to
relevant authorities as legally required. This is the single area with
zero tolerance and no warning step.

**Spam and deceptive content.** Misleading titles/thumbnails, repeated
near-duplicate uploads, undisclosed advertising, or content designed
purely to farm views/clicks rather than genuinely inform or entertain.

**Violence and graphic content.** Real violence, gore, or content
depicting serious harm without clear educational/documentary context and
appropriate warnings.

## How moderation works here

1. **Automated scan.** Every upload is scanned automatically immediately
   after upload completes. Content flagged by this scan is automatically
   rejected and never reaches human review.
2. **Human review.** Content that passes the automated scan enters the
   moderation queue, where a moderator approves or rejects it against
   these guidelines.
3. **Community reports.** Any signed-in user can report content they
   believe violates these guidelines. Reports go to a separate queue where
   a moderator can take down the content, leave it up, and optionally
   suspend the uploader's account for repeated or severe violations.

## Enforcement

First violations of most categories typically result in content removal.
Repeated violations may result in upload privileges being suspended or
account termination. Severe violations (especially anything endangering
minors) result in immediate permanent action without a warning step.
